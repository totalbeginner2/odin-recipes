Adobo

Pork adobo is a classic Filipino dish that is beloved for its rich, savory flavor and tender, juicy meat.
The dish is made by marinating chunks of pork in a mixture of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and black pepper,
then slow-cooking the meat in the marinade until it is fork-tender. The resulting dish is a delicious blend of salty, tangy,
and umami flavors that is perfect for spooning over rice.

In addition to its incredible taste, pork adobo is also known for its versatility. While pork is the most common protein used in this dish,
it can also be made with chicken, beef, or even fish. Additionally, the marinade can be adjusted to suit individual tastes,
with some recipes including ingredients like sugar, bay leaves, or chili peppers for added depth of flavor. Whether served as a hearty
main course or as a flavorful topping for sandwiches or salads, pork adobo is a delicious and comforting dish that is sure to satisfy.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 lbs pork shoulder
  • 1/4 cup cane vinegar or white vinegar
  • 1/3cup low sodium soy sauce
  • 6 cloves garlic (chopped)
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 teaspoons black peppercorns
  • 2 teaspoons sugar
  • 2 cups water

Steps

  1. In a medium dutch oven or pot over medium high heat, add the oil and sear the pork until browned on all sides.
  2. Add the vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, bay leaf, peppercorns (tied in cheese cloth if desired), sugar, and water, and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat to medium low, cover, and simmer for 1 hour.
  3. Remove the cover and continue simmering for another 30 minutes to reduce the sauce.
  4. Serve over rice!
